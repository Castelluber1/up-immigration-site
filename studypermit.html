FAQ

Do I always need a Study Permit to study in Canada?

In most cases yes! There are a few exceptions for short courses under 6 months, ESL, or if you already have legal status in Canada. If you are unsure, we can help you clarify your situation.

What documents do I need for a Study Permit?

You’ll need your Letter of Acceptance, proof of finances, passport, and immigration-specific forms. Our document specialist helps you list exactly what applies to your case so you don’t miss anything.

How much money do I need to show?

It depends on your tuition, location, and living costs. We help you calculate the amount you’ll need and gather proof in a way that makes sense to IRCC.

Can I work while studying in Canada?

Yes, many study permit holders are allowed to work part-time during school sessions and full-time during breaks. Also, some spouses may get work permits depending on program and location.

Can spouse or children study or work?

Yes, there are options. Spouses may get an open work permit depending on the program, and children may be eligible for study permits. We’ll verify for your particular case.
